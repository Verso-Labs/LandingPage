AI-native tools for teams that refuse to compromise

Agentic work should be an enabler, not a gadget on the side. We're building world-class AI models for teams that need to get value out of them. We're starting with slide work because it's the most painful and less efficient we've seen. We're not stopping there.

Why current models fall short

Most AI models generate HTML representations of slides—static images that look presentable but are virtually unusable. You can't edit text, adjust layouts, or apply brand guidelines. A presentation isn't a final output—it's a living document that gets refined and repurposed.

PowerPoint is a complex system of structured objects with properties, relationships, and constraints. Text boxes, shapes, charts, and layouts all follow strict rules inherited from master slides. Language models trained on natural language and code weren't built to manipulate nested XML schemas with visual constraints—that requires different intelligence.

Our approach: Native PowerPoint manipulation

At Verso, we've built the first system that truly works natively in PowerPoint. Our agents don't generate screenshots—they manipulate actual PowerPoint objects. They understand text boxes, shapes, SmartArt, charts, and tables. They respect your slide masters. They apply your color schemes. They work within your layout constraints.

This required building a specialized model and harness designed from the ground up for PowerPoint's object model. The result is slides you can actually use—edit text, swap images, adjust layouts, everything you'd expect from a native PowerPoint file.

Today, Verso has the best-performing model for native PowerPoint manipulation. But we're not stopping here. We're expanding beyond generation to full slide lifecycle management—iterative editing, version control, template learning, and multi-user collaboration.

Driven by curiosity, united by craft

We're constantly pushing the boundaries of what's possible. Researchers and builders work hand in hand—combining scientific rigor with product intuition.

Our research and machine learning teams are based in Paris, while our product team is based in New York. Together, we're shaping the future of agentic work that provides value.
